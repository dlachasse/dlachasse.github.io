A little bit about me

In the years since I graduated from high school I’ve had many thoughts about what I wanted to do with my life. In college I dabbled in subjects ranging from engineering to photography to film. My studies, life experiences and interests have helped me take a more honest perspective on my strengths — technical work.

About a year ago, I made a conscious effort to invest my time into learning a programming language. Around the same time, a friend approached me about helping him build an open source bill of materials tool for 3D printing projects. So we spent some time learning PHP and mashing the thing together (link). The language felt all wrong, though — the syntax made it surprisingly difficult to write readable code. So I decided to try another language out. I was interested in developing for the web and had heard a little about Rails. I found a couple books (rails tutorial and learn ruby the hard way) and gleaned enough information to build an RSS reader.

Previous work experience

I’ve spent the past 6 years working at a small online apparel retailer selling exclusively on Amazon. I’ve had the chance to do things from printing t-shirts to desiging a proprietary warehouse inventory system.

The position has provided me my first opportunity to program. When I first took the position I was tasked with building huge text inventory files in Excel and learned to automate simple actions with Visual Basic and slowly worked my way up to more complicated macros. Over time I’ve been able to create a number programs we use daily.

After learning enough Ruby I was able to put together a utility to assist with some inventory synchronization issues that had been plaguing the business. I also built a couple other utilities that do some web scraping as well as log and analyze pricing changes on Amazon.
